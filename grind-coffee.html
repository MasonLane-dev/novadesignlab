The Grind Coffee — Cafe Website

A warm, inviting digital presence designed to bring the cafe experience online — with online menus, subscription plans, and a seamless path from browse to visit.

Client: The Grind Coffee (Concept Project) Timeline: 2 weeks Role: Design & Development Category: Cafe Website

Overview

The Grind Coffee is an independent cafe concept based in Cardiff, built around the idea of specialty coffee in a warm, community-focused setting. The project brief was to create a website that captures the cafe's cosy atmosphere, makes their menu and story easy to find, and encourages both in-person visits and online coffee subscriptions.

The Problem

Many independent cafes rely on social media alone for their online presence. While Instagram drives foot traffic, it doesn't provide a central place for customers to find opening hours, browse the full menu, learn the backstory, or sign up for a subscription service. The Grind Coffee needed a proper website — not just another social page — to act as the digital front door for the business.

Key challenges included:

  • No existing website — starting from scratch, with no brand guidelines beyond a visual concept.
  • Dual audience — the site needed to serve both local customers (finding the cafe, viewing the menu) and online customers (subscription coffee delivery).
  • Warmth vs usability — balancing a cosy, artisanal aesthetic with clear navigation and calls to action.

The Solution

I designed a warm, inviting brand presence using a rich colour palette of browns, creams, and purples — inspired by coffee, steamed milk, and the cafe's signature lavender latte. The typography pairs a serif heading font (Playfair Display) for a handcrafted feel with a clean sans-serif (Inter) for readability.

The site is structured around a clear narrative:

  • A hero section with atmospheric imagery, a tagline, and two CTAs — one for visiting, one for ordering online.
  • An about section that tells the story behind the cafe, building an emotional connection with visitors.
  • A full menu page with categories (coffee, food, specials) — designed for scanning, not reading.
  • A subscription page with tiered coffee plans, making it easy for customers to sign up for regular deliveries.
  • A contact & location section with Google Maps integration, hours, and a contact form.

Key design decision: Rather than hiding the menu behind a PDF download (common for cafes), I built the menu directly into the page as styled cards. This makes it mobile-friendly, indexable by Google, and instantly accessible without a download step.

Tech Stack

  • HTML5 — semantic structure for accessibility and SEO
  • CSS3 — custom styles with CSS animations, flexbox, and grid
  • JavaScript — mobile menu toggle, testimonial carousel, scroll reveal animations
  • Google Fonts — Playfair Display + Inter for typography
  • Google Maps API — embedded interactive map for the location section

The site is fully static — no framework overhead, no build tools. This means it loads fast, scores well on Lighthouse, and is easy to hand off to a client for basic content updates.

Results

As a concept/template project, The Grind Coffee website demonstrates the kind of results a local cafe can expect from a professional web presence:

  • Instant credibility — a proper website signals professionalism far beyond a social media page alone.
  • Mobile-first experience — critical for customers searching for cafes on the go.
  • Clear conversion paths — every page has a purpose: visit, order, subscribe, or contact.
  • Local SEO foundation — structured content makes it easy for Google to index and rank for local searches like "Cardiff cafe" or "specialty coffee Cardiff."

What I'd do differently for a real client: Add online ordering integration (Square/Stripe), Google Business Profile sync, and a loyalty programme feature. The template provides a solid foundation that can be extended based on the client's specific needs.
