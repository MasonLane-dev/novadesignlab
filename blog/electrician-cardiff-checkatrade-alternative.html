The Cardiff Electrician's Guide to Getting More Work Without Checkatrade

Here's how the Checkatrade model works, step by step.

A Cardiff homeowner needs an electrician. They go to Checkatrade, type in their job, and submit a request. That request goes to you — and to three or four other Cardiff electricians. You each put in a quote. The homeowner compares them, usually on price, and picks the lowest one that still looks credible enough.

You might win it. You might not. Either way, you're competing on price, on a platform you pay £800 to £1,500 a year to be on, against people who do the same job as you, for a lead that was never exclusively yours.

Month after month. Every job.

That's not a business model. That's an auction. And Checkatrade is the auctioneer taking a cut regardless of who wins.

There's another way.

What's Actually Happening When a Cardiff Homeowner Searches for an Electrician

When someone in Cardiff needs an electrician, most of them do the same thing: they open their phone and type "electrician Cardiff" or "local electrician Cardiff" into Google.

What they see first is the Map Pack — three local businesses shown at the top of the results, with a map, star ratings, and a click-to-call button. Those three spots get the bulk of the clicks. After that come the organic results: individual business websites, Checkatrade, Yell, and a few others.

Checkatrade does appear in those results. But here's what most electricians don't realise: individual websites appear right alongside them — sometimes above them. A well-built local website, with the right setup, can rank in the same positions as platforms with enormous marketing budgets. Not because it outmuscles them, but because Google's local search algorithm rewards relevance and proximity over platform size.

When your website shows up for "electrician Cardiff" and a homeowner clicks through to your site, they're not comparing you with three other people. They're looking at you. If your site answers their questions, shows your credentials, and makes it easy to call — you're the one they ring.

That's a fundamentally different situation to a Checkatrade lead. The homeowner came to find you specifically. They've already started trusting you before they've even picked up the phone.

What Your Checkatrade Spend Could Buy Instead

Let's put some numbers on this.

A Checkatrade sole trader subscription runs from around £800 to £1,500 a year, depending on your package and trade category. Some electricians pay more. That's before your time — the hours spent building a profile, chasing reviews to keep the rating up, writing quotes that may or may not win, and managing the platform's admin.

A professionally built website for an electrician in Cardiff costs more upfront than a year's Checkatrade subscription. But after that, it's yours. You own it. No annual renewal to maintain access to your own leads. No algorithm changes that affect how prominently you're shown. No platform that can decide tomorrow to promote whoever bids highest for visibility.

The leads that come through your website are exclusive. A homeowner searched, found you, clicked your site, read about you, and decided to call. They didn't submit a request that went to five people — they called you. The conversion rate on those enquiries is dramatically higher, and the conversations start from a different place. Price is rarely the first question when someone has already chosen you.

One extra job a month from your website — at average Cardiff electrician rates for a decent-sized job — can pay for the site's build cost within a few months. After that, it's pure upside. Checkatrade requires you to keep paying to keep receiving. Your website keeps working whether you're paying attention to it or not.

What a Cardiff Electrician's Website Needs to Actually Rank

Not all websites generate leads. Most trade websites don't rank at all — they were built to look professional, not to be found. Here's the difference.

Your credentials on the homepage, immediately visible. NICEIC or NAPIT registration badges should be in the header or at the very top of the page. Cardiff homeowners searching for an electrician are often thinking about safety — whether the person they hire is qualified and registered. Show it immediately. Don't make them hunt for it.

Your trade and location in the main heading. Something like "Electrician in Cardiff — Domestic, Commercial & EV Charging" tells Google exactly what you do and where. It also tells the homeowner they've landed in the right place in the first second.

Separate pages for each service. Consumer unit upgrades. EICR certificates. EV charger installation. Rewires. Fuse board repairs. Each one as its own page, each one with Cardiff mentioned in the content. EV charger installation in particular is a growing search term right now — Cardiff homeowners are buying electric cars and looking for a local electrician to install a home charger.

A Google Business Profile that matches your website exactly. Your business name, address, phone number, and service categories need to be identical across your website and your GBP. Inconsistencies confuse Google and suppress your local ranking.

Reviews that mention Cardiff areas by name. A Google review that says "brilliant electrician, did our consumer unit upgrade in Roath" does more for your local ranking than one that just says "great job." When you're following up on a completed job and asking for a review — you can politely mention that it helps if they include what the job was and where.

What Happens When You Own Your Leads

The shift that happens when a Cardiff electrician starts generating their own enquiries through Google is hard to describe until you experience it. But here's the shape of it.

You set the price. When a customer found you specifically — not through a comparison platform — they're not expecting to negotiate you down. They've already decided you're their electrician.

You choose the jobs. When you have consistent enquiries coming in, you can be selective. You can prioritise the higher-value jobs, the repeat customers, the commercial work.

One job becomes many. A homeowner who found you on Google, had a good experience, and has your number saved will call you again. They'll recommend you to neighbours. The original lead was free — it came from your website ranking — and it keeps compounding.

No platform risk. Checkatrade has changed its pricing before. It's shared leads with more electricians before. Any of those things can happen again without warning. Your website doesn't change the rules on you. Your Google ranking, once earned, is yours to maintain.

The Electrician Who Shows Up on Google Gets the Call

Every day in Cardiff, homeowners search "local electrician Cardiff" and call whoever comes up first. They're not thinking about Checkatrade. They're Googling, clicking the first credible result, and calling.

Right now, that first result probably isn't you. It's a competitor with a website that was built to rank — or it's Checkatrade, taking their slice.
