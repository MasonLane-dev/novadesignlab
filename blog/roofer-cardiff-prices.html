Typical Roofing Costs in Cardiff: 2026 Price Guide

Roofing prices vary more than almost any other trade. The size of the job, the pitch of the roof, the type of materials, and how accessible the site is all affect the final figure. These ranges are a realistic starting point — not a fixed quote.

Minor Tile Repairs (1–5 tiles)

Replacing a handful of broken or slipped tiles is usually a small job. Most Cardiff roofers will charge a callout fee plus labour, typically landing between £150 and £350 for a straightforward repair on an accessible roof.

Larger Tile Work (section of a roof)

If you've got a section that's lifted, been storm-damaged, or had the felt underneath fail, you're looking at a more involved job. Expect to pay somewhere between £500 and £1,500 depending on scope and how much scaffolding is required.

Flat Roof Repair

Felt flat roofs, common on extensions and garage roofs across Cardiff, can fail in sections. A patch repair might come in at £300–£600. A full felt replacement on a single-garage-size flat roof typically runs £800–£1,500. EPDM rubber roofing — a more durable alternative — will cost more upfront but lasts significantly longer.

Full Pitched Roof Re-Roof

This is a big job. A full strip-and-retile on a standard semi-detached in Cardiff, with scaffolding, new battens, felt, and tiles, typically falls between £5,000 and £12,000. The size of the property, tile type (concrete vs. Welsh slate), and access all move that figure significantly. Get three quotes.

Fascias, Soffits, and Guttering

Replacing the fascia boards, soffits, and gutters on a typical Cardiff terrace or semi usually costs between £800 and £2,500, depending on the number of elevations and whether the existing boards are timber or uPVC. Guttering alone — cleaning, resealing joints, or replacing sections — is typically £100–£400.

Chimney Work

Repointing a chimney stack: £300–£700. Full chimney rebuild: £2,000–£5,000+. Get it done while the scaffold is up for something else and you'll save on access costs.

What Separates a £400 Job from a £4,000 Job

Two homeowners can call a roofer in Cardiff with the same problem — a leaking roof — and end up with very different quotes. Here's why.

Access. If your roof needs scaffolding to reach safely, that's a cost before anyone has touched a single tile. Scaffolding on a Cardiff terrace might add £500–£800 to a job. Some roofers can use a tower or ladder for limited access work; others won't work without full scaffold. Both can be the right call depending on the job.

What's underneath. A tile replacement looks simple until you lift the tile and find the felt underneath has rotted, or the batten has split, or there's condensation damage to the timber. A roofer who spots that and tells you upfront is doing their job properly. A roofer who doesn't mention it — or who quotes low and then adds it on — is a different story.

Material choice. Concrete tiles are the most affordable. Welsh slate is more expensive but lasts longer and suits older Cardiff properties aesthetically. Natural slate, reclaimed slate, and premium tiles each carry their own price point. The right choice depends on your budget, your property's age, and whether you're matching existing tiles on a heritage or conservation-area home.

The roofer themselves. A sole trader with low overheads, working a regular Cardiff patch, often quotes differently to a larger company with a fleet of vans and premises. Neither is automatically better or worse — but it's worth understanding who you're dealing with before you compare numbers on paper.

How to Find a Good Roofer in Cardiff

There's no shortage of people offering roofing work in Cardiff. Sorting the good from the not-so-good before you've spent any money comes down to a few things.

Check for NFRC registration. The National Federation of Roofing Contractors is the trade body for UK roofers. Membership means a roofer has been vetted and agreed to a code of professional practice.

Look at their website — specifically their photos. A Cardiff roofer worth hiring should have photos of work they've done on Cardiff properties. Not stock images — actual jobs with visible street context.

Read reviews, and check the dates. Five-star reviews from four years ago don't tell you much. Look for recent reviews — ideally from the last 12 months — that mention specific streets or areas of Cardiff.

Red flags to watch for: No website at all. Reviews that are vague or all posted within a short window. Cash-only payment or pressure to decide on the spot. Quotes provided without anyone actually looking at the roof.
